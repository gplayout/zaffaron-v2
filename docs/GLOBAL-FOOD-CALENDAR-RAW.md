## 1. Major Religious Observances

### Eid al-Fitr (عيد الفطر)
- **Local Name:** عيد الفطر (Arabic), Ramazan Bayramı (Turkish), Eid-e Fetr (Persian)
- **Date Type:** lunar_hijri
- **Date Rule:** Shawwal 1-3 (1st day after Ramadan ends)
- **Regions:** Global Muslim communities (Middle East, South Asia, Southeast Asia, Africa, diaspora worldwide)
- **Cuisines:** Arabic, Persian, Turkish, South Asian, Malay, Indonesian, African
- **Signature Dishes:** 
  - Sheer Khurma (شیر خورما) - Vermicelli pudding with dates and milk
  - Ma'amoul (معمول) - Date-filled semolina cookies
  - Samosas - Fried pastries with spiced fillings
  - Biryani - Layered rice and meat dish
  - Kahk (كحك) - Egyptian Eid cookies
- **Cultural Context:** Eid al-Fitr marks the end of Ramadan fasting. Sweet dishes symbolize the sweetness of breaking the fast and celebrating spiritual renewal. Dates are universally significant as they're traditionally used to break the daily fast during Ramadan. Families prepare elaborate feasts and share food with neighbors and the poor.
- **Search Keywords:** Eid al-Fitr food, Eid recipes, Sheer Khurma recipe, Ramadan Eid dishes, traditional Eid food
- **Tier:** 1

### Eid al-Adha (عيد الأضحى)
- **Local Name:** عيد الأضحى (Arabic), Kurban Bayramı (Turkish), Eid-e Ghorban (Persian)
- **Date Type:** lunar_hijri
- **Date Rule:** Dhu al-Hijjah 10-13
- **Regions:** Global Muslim communities
- **Cuisines:** Arabic, Persian, Turkish, South Asian, African
- **Signature Dishes:**
  - Qurbani meat dishes - Various preparations of sacrificial lamb/goat
  - Mansaf (منسف) - Jordanian/Palestinian lamb and yogurt rice
  - Kabsa (كبسة) - Saudi spiced rice with meat
  - Nihari - Slow-cooked beef stew (South Asia)
  - Fatta (فتة) - Egyptian bread and meat dish
- **Cultural Context:** The Feast of Sacrifice commemorates Prophet Ibrahim's willingness to sacrifice his son. Families sacrifice an animal (usually sheep, goat, or cow) and distribute meat to family, friends, and the poor. Meat-centered dishes dominate as the sacrifice is central to the celebration. Each culture has traditional ways of honoring the blessed meat.
- **Search Keywords:** Eid al-Adha recipes, qurbani meat dishes, Eid sacrifice food, Bakra Eid recipes, traditional Eid al-Adha dishes
- **Tier:** 1

### Ramadan (رمضان)
- **Local Name:** رمضان كريم (Arabic), Ramazan (Turkish), Ramazan (Persian/Afghan)
- **Date Type:** lunar_hijri
- **Date Rule:** Entire month of Ramadan (9th month)
- **Regions:** Global Muslim communities
- **Cuisines:** All Islamic cuisines
- **Signature Dishes:**
  - Dates and water - Traditional iftar opener
  - Haleem (حلیم) - Wheat and meat porridge
  - Harira (حريرة) - Moroccan lentil and tomato soup
  - Pakoras - Fried fritters (South Asia)
  - Kunafa (كنافة) - Cheese pastry with syrup
- **Cultural Context:** The holy month of fasting from dawn to sunset transforms food culture. Iftar (breaking fast) becomes a communal celebration with specific ritual foods. Dates hold religious significance as the Prophet Muhammad broke his fast with them. Pre-dawn suhoor meals sustain fasters through the day. Mosques and communities organize massive iftar gatherings.
- **Search Keywords:** Iftar recipes, Ramadan food, suhoor ideas, Ramadan special dishes, iftar menu ideas
- **Tier:** 1

### Nowruz (نوروز)
- **Local Name:** نوروز (Persian), Navruz (Uzbek), Nevruz (Turkish), Nauruz (Afghan)
- **Date Type:** solar
- **Date Rule:** March 20-21 (vernal equinox)
- **Regions:** Iran, Afghanistan, Central Asia (Uzbekistan, Tajikistan, Azerbaijan), Kurdish regions, Balkans
- **Cuisines:** Persian, Afghan, Central Asian, Kurdish
- **Signature Dishes:**
  - Sabzi Polo Mahi (سبزی پلو ماهی) - Herb rice with fish
  - Samanu (سمنو) - Sprouted wheat pudding
  - Ash Reshteh (آش رشته) - Noodle and herb soup
  - Haft Sin table foods - Seven symbolic items including apples, sumac, vinegar
  - Sumalak (сумалак) - Uzbek wheat sprout pudding
- **Cultural Context:** The Persian New Year celebrating spring's arrival is one of humanity's oldest continuous celebrations (3,000+ years). The Haft Sin table displays seven items starting with 'S' in Persian, each symbolizing renewal and prosperity. Fish represents life, greens represent rebirth, and sweets bring sweetness for the new year. Families gather for elaborate feasts marking rebirth and hope.
- **Search Keywords:** Nowruz food, Haft Sin table, Sabzi Polo recipe, Nowruz traditions, Persian New Year dishes
- **Tier:** 1

## 2. Persian & Afghan Holidays

- **Local Name:** چهارشنبه‌سوری (Persian), Red Wednesday
- **Date Type:** solar
- **Date Rule:** Last Tuesday night before Nowruz (March)
- **Regions:** Iran, Afghanistan, Kurdish regions, Azerbaijan
- **Cuisines:** Persian, Kurdish, Afghan
- **Signature Dishes:**
  - Aash (آش) - Thick noodle and herb soup
  - Ajil-e Chaharshanbe Suri - Mixed nuts and dried fruits
  - Reshteh Polo (رشته پلو) - Rice with noodles
  - Sanganak (سنگانک) - Charcoal-roasted nuts
  - Ash-e Reshteh - Specific version for the night
- **Cultural Context:** The ancient fire-jumping festival purifies people before the new year. Nuts and dried fruits are shared while families gather around bonfires. The aash (soup) tradition ensures a warm, hearty meal during the outdoor celebrations. The mixed nuts (ajil) represent the abundance hoped for in the coming year. This Zoroastrian-origin celebration predates Islam in Persia.
- **Search Keywords:** Chaharshanbe Suri food, Nowruz preparation, Persian fire festival food, aash recipe
- **Tier:** 2

### Ethiopian Meskel (መስቀል)
- **Local Name:** መስቀል (Meskel)
- **Date Type:** solar (Ethiopian calendar)
- **Date Rule:** September 27 (or 28 leap years)
- **Regions:** Ethiopia, Eritrea
- **Cuisines:** Ethiopian
- **Signature Dishes:**
  - Injera - Sour flatbread (essential)
  - Doro wat - Spicy chicken stew
  - Kitfo - Minced raw beef (regional)
  - Tibs - Sautéed meat
  - Tej - Honey wine
- **Cultural Context:** The Finding of the True Cross is celebrated with massive bonfires (demera) similar to ancient fire traditions. After church ceremonies, families share large communal meals centered on injera and spicy stews. The food reflects Ethiopian Orthodox fasting calendar practices, with Meskel often being a feasting break from fasting periods. Sharing from one platter symbolizes community.
- **Search Keywords:** Meskel food, Ethiopian Orthodox festival, Doro wat recipe, Injera tradition
- **Tier:** 2

### Ethiopian Timkat (ጥምቀት)
- **Local Name:** ጥምቀት (Timkat)
- **Date Type:** solar (Ethiopian calendar)
- **Date Rule:** January 19 (or 20 leap years)
- **Regions:** Ethiopia, Eritrea
- **Cuisines:** Ethiopian
- **Signature Dishes:**
  - Injera with wats - Various stews
  - Doro wat - Special feast dish
  - Tella - Traditional beer
  - Genfo - Barley porridge
  - Coffee ceremony foods
- **Cultural Context:** The Ethiopian Orthodox Epiphany involves reenactments of Christ's baptism. After all-night celebrations and water blessings, families feast. Doro wat is reserved for major celebrations due to chicken's expense. The coffee ceremony (buna) is integral, with popcorn and bread served. The food celebrates spiritual renewal through communal eating.
- **Search Keywords:** Timkat food, Ethiopian Epiphany, Doro wat feast, Ethiopian coffee ceremony
- **Tier:** 2

### Nigerian Independence Day
- **Local Name:** Independence Day
- **Date Type:** gregorian_fixed
- **Date Rule:** October 1
- **Regions:** Nigeria
- **Cuisines:** Nigerian (Yoruba, Igbo, Hausa)
- **Signature Dishes:**
  - Jollof rice - West African rice dish
  - Suya - Spiced grilled meat
  - Egusi soup - Melon seed stew
  - Pounded yam and soup
  - Moi moi - Steamed bean pudding
- **Cultural Context:** Nigeria's independence from Britain (1960) is celebrated with the nation's most iconic foods. Jollof rice represents West African culinary competition (Nigeria vs Ghana). Suya reflects northern Hausa influences. The spread emphasizes Nigeria's ethnic diversity through foods from different regions. Large parties and street celebrations feature abundant food.
- **Search Keywords:** Nigerian Independence Day food, Jollof rice recipe, Suya recipe, Nigerian national dishes
- **Tier:** 2

### Moroccan Eid al-Fitr
- **Local Name:** عيد الفطر
- **Date Type:** lunar_hijri
- **Date Rule:** Shawwal 1
- **Regions:** Morocco
- **Cuisines:** Moroccan
- **Signature Dishes:**
  - Chebakia - Sesame honey cookies
  - Sellou (sfouf) - Roasted flour and nut mixture
  - Harira - Lentil soup
  - Briouats - Fried pastries
  - Mint tea
- **Cultural Context:** Moroccan Eid emphasizes elaborate sweets like chebakia and sellou, prepared in huge quantities to offer guests. These sweets provide energy after a month of fasting. Harira continues from Ramadan as a comforting soup. Hospitality is paramount, with families hosting open houses. The abundance of sweets symbolizes joy and celebration.
- **Search Keywords:** Moroccan Eid food, Chebakia recipe, Sellou recipe, Moroccan sweets Eid
- **Tier:** 2

### Somali Eid al-Fitr
- **Local Name:** Ciidul Fidriga
- **Date Type:** lunar_hijri
- **Date Rule:** Shawwal 1
- **Regions:** Somalia, Somali diaspora
- **Cuisines:** Somali
- **Signature Dishes:**
  - Bariis iskukaris - Spiced rice
  - Suqaar - Diced meat sauté
  - Canjeero - Fermented flatbread
  - Xalwo (halwa) - Sweet confection
  - Sambusa - Somali samosas
- **Cultural Context:** Somali Eid features aromatic rice dishes and abundant sweets. Xalwo is essential, made in large trays and shared with neighbors. Families wear new clothes and visit relatives, bringing sweets. Sambusas continue from Ramadan traditions. The cuisine reflects Somali trade links with Arabia and India through spices.
- **Search Keywords:** Somali Eid food, Xalwo recipe, Bariis iskukaris, Somali sambusa
- **Tier:** 2

## 12. Seasonal & Harvest Festivals

### Oktoberfest
- **Local Name:** Oktoberfest
- **Date Type:** variable
- **Date Rule:** Late September - first weekend of October
- **Regions:** Germany (Munich), global
- **Cuisines:** Bavarian/German
- **Signature Dishes:**
  - Pretzels (Brezn)
  - Bratwurst
  - Roast chicken (Hendl)
  - Sauerkraut
  - Beer
- **Cultural Context:** The beer festival began as a royal wedding celebration in 1810 and became an annual harvest-time gathering. Bavarian foods are designed to accompany heavy beer drinking. The festival celebrates German brewing culture and autumn harvest. International versions spread German food culture worldwide.
- **Search Keywords:** Oktoberfest food, Bavarian recipes, pretzel recipe, Oktoberfest traditional dishes
- **Tier:** 2

### Thanksgiving (US)
- **Local Name:** Thanksgiving
- **Date Type:** variable
- **Date Rule:** Fourth Thursday of November
- **Regions:** United States
- **Cuisines:** American
- **Signature Dishes:**
  - Roast turkey
  - Stuffing/dressing
  - Cranberry sauce

Indigenous foods like corn and cranberries appear symbolically, though the holiday's history is contested. Modern celebrations center on a large family meal and sharing.
- **Search Keywords:** Thanksgiving dinner menu, turkey recipe, stuffing recipe, Thanksgiving sides
- **Tier:** 1

### Lunar New Year (Global)
- **Local Name:** Various (Chinese New Year, Tet, Seollal)
- **Date Type:** lunar_chinese
- **Date Rule:** First day of lunar year
- **Regions:** East and Southeast Asia
- **Cuisines:** Chinese, Vietnamese, Korean
- **Signature Dishes:**
  - Dumplings
  - Rice cakes
  - Whole fish
  - Sticky rice cakes
  - Sweet rice balls
- **Cultural Context:** The lunar new year across Asia shares themes of family reunion, ancestor worship, and symbolic foods for prosperity. Each culture has specific foods but common symbolism includes roundness (unity) and long noodles (longevity). Food gifting and communal meals are central.
- **Search Keywords:** Lunar New Year food, Chinese New Year dishes, Tet food, Seollal food
- **Tier:** 1
