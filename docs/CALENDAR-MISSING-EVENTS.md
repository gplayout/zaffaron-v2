### Mawlid al-Nabi / Prophet’s Birthday (المولد النبوي)
- **Local Name:** المولد النبوي (Arabic), Mevlid Kandili (Turkish), Milad-un-Nabi (Urdu)
- **Date Type:** lunar_hijri
- **Date Rule:** Rabiʿ al-Awwal 12 (some communities observe 17)
- **Regions:** Muslim communities worldwide (especially Middle East, North/West Africa, South Asia, Southeast Asia)
- **Cuisines:** Arab, Turkish, North African, South Asian, Malay/Indonesian
- **Signature Dishes:**
  - Halawat al-mولد / حلويات المولد (Egypt) - assorted sesame and nut sweets
  - Zarda (زردہ) - sweet saffron rice (South Asia)
  - Sheer khurma (شیر خرما) - vermicelli milk pudding
  - Mevlid lokumu - Turkish lokum sweets served at gatherings
  - Sellou (سلو) - Moroccan nut and flour confection
- **Cultural Context:** Mawlid commemorates the birth of the Prophet Muhammad and is marked in many communities with recitations, gatherings, charity, and sweets. Foods emphasize generosity and communal sharing, often distributed to neighbors and the poor.
- **Search Keywords:** Mawlid food, Milad un Nabi sweets, Mevlid Kandili treats, halawet el molid
- **Tier:** 1

### Laylat al-Qadr / Night of Power (ليلة القدر)
- **Local Name:** ليلة القدر (Arabic), Kadir Gecesi (Turkish), شب قدر (Persian/Urdu)
- **Date Type:** lunar_hijri
- **Date Rule:** One of the last 10 nights of Ramadan, commonly 27 Ramadan
- **Regions:** Muslim communities worldwide
- **Cuisines:** All Islamic cuisines
- **Signature Dishes:**
  - Dates and laban - simple iftar staples
  - Harira (حريرة) - Moroccan soup for Ramadan nights
  - Qatayef (قطايف) - stuffed pancakes (Levant)
  - Haleem (حلیم) - slow-cooked wheat and meat porridge
  - Kunafa (كنافة) - syrup-soaked pastry
- **Cultural Context:** Considered the holiest night in Ramadan, devoted to prayer and reflection. Many families keep food simple but sustaining for late-night worship, with shared iftars at mosques and community centers.
- **Search Keywords:** Laylat al-Qadr iftar, Night of Power food, Ramadan last ten nights
- **Tier:** 1

- **Date Type:** lunar_hijri
- **Date Rule:** Rajab 27
- **Regions:** Muslim communities worldwide (commonly observed in Turkey, Levant, South Asia)

- **Search Keywords:** Isra Miraj food, Miraç Kandili kandil simidi, kandil sweets

### Ashura (Sunni observance) / 10 Muharram (عاشوراء)
- **Local Name:** عاشوراء (Arabic), Aşure Günü (Turkish)
- **Date Type:** lunar_hijri
- **Date Rule:** Muharram 10 (fasting often on 9–10 or 10–11)
- **Regions:** Muslim communities worldwide (Sunni)
- **Cuisines:** Turkish, Arab, South Asian
- **Signature Dishes:**
  - Aşure (aşure tatlısı) - Turkish “Noah’s pudding”
  - Haleem (حلیم) - communal porridge (South Asia)

  - Dates and water - breaking fast

- **Search Keywords:** Ashura Sunni fasting food, aşure pudding, 10 Muharram fast
